Project Introduction: No More

This interactive report demonstrates the fulfillment of key objectives within the Epitech Innovative Project (EIP) Solution Track. Our project, "No More," is an innovative mobile application designed to provide comprehensive support and resources to individuals battling addiction. It also aims to help users get in touch with professionals for further assistance. By fostering a community-driven approach to recovery, "No More" aims to create a tangible societal impact, focusing on a well-crafted user experience, an effective visibility strategy, and an engaged, supportive community. This document outlines our strategies and showcases the evidence of our achievements.

Beta Tester Reward Strategy (Non-Monetary)

Given our current resource constraints, we've designed a simple yet effective reward system for our beta testers that focuses on recognition and influence:

  • Early Access & Exclusive Features: Beta testers will be the first to experience new features and improvements before they are released to the wider public, giving them a privileged status.
  • Direct Influence on Development: We will actively solicit and prioritize feedback from our beta testers. Their suggestions and bug reports will directly shape the future development of "No More," giving them a sense of ownership and impact.
  • Public Recognition (Optional): With their consent, top contributors and most active beta testers will be publicly acknowledged on our social media channels or within the app itself (e.g., in a "Special Thanks" section), providing valuable exposure and recognition.
  • Community Leadership: Opportunities will be created for highly engaged beta testers to become community moderators or mentors within the app, fostering a sense of leadership and responsibility.

This strategy aims to build a loyal and highly engaged beta community by valuing their contributions and making them integral to the project's success.

2. Working on User Experience (UX/UI)

We are committed to a user-centered design process, ensuring our application truly meets the needs of its users. This commitment is guided by our comprehensive graphic charter.

User-Centered Design

  • We conduct **direct, hand-to-hand user testing** to validate assumptions about ergonomics, usability, and user needs in real-world scenarios. This direct interaction allows us to gather rich, qualitative feedback, observing user behavior firsthand as they interact with "No More."
  • Based on the invaluable feedback gathered from these tests, we continuously iterate on the interface and overall user experience, ensuring it evolves with our users' preferences and pain points, making the app more intuitive and effective.

High-Quality Prototyping

Our aim is to deliver a product with an intuitive and visually appealing interface that resonates with our audience. We implement modern UI/UX principles, including responsive design for optimal viewing across devices, simplicity in navigation, and accessibility features to ensure "No More" is usable by everyone. Our design consistency is maintained through:

  • Typography: We utilize the **Ubuntu** font family for all text to ensure readability across devices. Key guidelines include using "Ubuntu Bold" for headlines (sentence case, optical kerning, slightly negative tracking) and "Ubuntu Regular" or "Ubuntu Light" for body text (sentence case, optical kerning, slightly negative tracking). All text is aligned to the left with a minimum margin of 3px.
  • Illustrations & Icons: Our visual assets are designed to be clean, modern, and convey support and clarity, using simple geometric shapes, clear lines, and a limited color palette derived from the NoMore brand colors. They provide symbolism and conceptual clarity, always aligning with our empathetic and hopeful tone.
  • Photography: Photography used within the app and marketing materials will be authentic, empathetic, and inspiring, focusing on real, diverse, and relatable people. We use natural lighting, warm tones, and a sense of calm, while strictly avoiding overly dramatic, negative, or misconstrued images.

Seamless Experience

  • We are dedicated to minimizing friction during use, focusing on efficient loading times and intuitive navigation paths to ensure a smooth user journey.
  • To further support our users and ensure a truly seamless experience, we provide clear and comprehensive user documentation, including easily accessible in-app guides and a detailed FAQ section. This empowers users to quickly find answers and maximize their engagement with "No More."

Objective A: Increasing Visibility and Impact on Social Media

Our strategy for social media visibility and impact centers on creating highly engaging and shareable content across Instagram (`@nomore_eip`) and LinkedIn (`@nomorepro`).

Optimized Content Strategy

For **Instagram**, we will develop a content calendar focusing on emotionally resonant themes, personal stories of recovery (anonymized where necessary), and interactive elements like polls, Q&As, and live sessions. We aim to leverage trending audio and visual formats to increase discoverability. Contests and collaborative campaigns with health and wellness advocates will be designed to encourage user-generated content and wider sharing, driving virality. We will strategically utilize hashtags such as `#DependenceFree` and `#NoMore` to expand our reach and connect with relevant communities.

On **LinkedIn**, our strategy will emphasize professional credibility and thought leadership. We plan to share updates on app development, insights into addiction recovery research, and announcements of new partnerships. Our content will be tailored to engage healthcare professionals, policymakers, and potential organizational partners. We will actively participate in relevant industry discussions and showcase "No More" as a vital solution in digital health. We will also incorporate relevant professional hashtags to maximize visibility within the industry.
